# 9-Block Vision Processing — Streamax Discovery

Run AFTER the buyer admits a pain. Grid = 3 question types x 3 areas. Aim for a
3:1 ratio of control to open questions.

## Question types
- **Open** — invite them to talk ("Tell me why you think you're missing your
  safety-incident targets"). Earns the right to ask control questions.
- **Control** — specific, quantifying ("Is it because you can't catch fatigue
  before an event? How often? What does one at-fault accident cost you all-in —
  claim + downtime + premium impact?"). This is where value gets measured.
- **Confirm** — play it back ("So the reasons are X and Y, costing ~$N/yr —
  right?").

## The three rows (do them in order)
1. **Diagnose reasons** — why the pain exists. Drill down and put a number on it.
2. **Explore impact** — who else is hit (Safety Mgr -> Ops -> CFO insurance line
   -> CEO growth story). This is where the deal gets bigger and power gets found.
3. **Visualize capabilities** — get THEM to describe the fix in capability terms,
   not product names, and own it: "What if the AI flagged fatigue 15 min before
   eyes close and cut false alerts 90% — would incidents drop? By how much?"

## Close the model with a buyer-owned vision
"If you had the ability to (A) + (B), you could (goal = inverse of the pain)." Get
a yes. That vision is what you later confirm in the Power Sponsor Letter.

## Coaching a rep
Ask them: what pain is admitted? Give me 3 control questions that quantify it.
Who else in the org does it touch? What Streamax capability knocks down each
reason? Then draft the confirming vision statement for their actual deal.
