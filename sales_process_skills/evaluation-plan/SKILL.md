# Evaluation Plan + Power Sponsor Letter — Streamax

You control the buying PROCESS (not the buyer). This is the best closing tool.

## Power Sponsor Letter (send with the plan)
Recap, in the buyer's language: the pain, the reasons, the buying vision (Box C3),
the organizational impact (Pain Chain), and their agreement to explore. This
proves you understand the business and locks in commitments.

## Draft Evaluation Plan (attach it)
A sequence of interdependent events, each with: Event, Week-of date, Owner, and a
mutual **Go/No-Go** decision point. Typical events for a fleet deal:
- Diagnose + confirm findings to top management
- Reference visit / pilot install on N trucks
- Value justification (buyer's numbers) + payback
- Legal / IT / procurement clearance
- Pre-proposal review (buyer edits the proposal = buyer owns it)
- Decision + Success Criteria to measure post-sale

**Send a DRAFT.** When the buyer edits it, they own it. A buyer who won't invest
time in the plan isn't serious — a fast qualifier.

## Milestones (grade the deal)
T -> S (Suspect) -> D (Sponsor Letter exists) -> C (at power, pain+vision+plan
agreed) -> B (Decision Due) -> A (Pending Sale) -> W. Grade by the last verifiable
outcome, not optimism.
