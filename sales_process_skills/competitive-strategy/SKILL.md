# Competitive Strategy (when you're not first) — Streamax

First: **Opportunity Assessment.** Is it active? Do you have access to power, the
power person's pain, a vision match, quantified value, and control? Bad odds ->
fold early.

## Pick a strategy
- **Head-to-Head** — ONLY if you're 2-10x stronger in the CUSTOMER's eyes.
  Feature-matching an entrenched incumbent usually loses.
- **End-Around (default when not first)** — don't fight on their requirements;
  CHANGE them. First make yourself equal (understand their current vision), then
  make yourself different. E.g. shift a Samsara-spec RFP from "dashcam features +
  price" to "behavioral-AI outcomes, 90% false-alert reduction, in-timezone local
  support, and 3-year TCO including the tracker you no longer need." When the
  competitor accepts the new field, they're outplayed.
- **Divide & Conquer** — win a beachhead (one region / trailer-cam line) and
  expand from inside.
- **Stall** — slow them down (roadmap preannounce) when you can't win today.

## Differentiator grid
List differentiators; rate each on uniqueness (0-10) and value-to-customer (0-10).
Upper-right (unique + valuable) — SafeGPT behavioral AI, native CAN/OBD, local
teams in 8 countries, white-label economics, 25-37% below Samsara — become the
MUST-HAVE requirements you write into the buyer's evaluation.
