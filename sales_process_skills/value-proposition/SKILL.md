# Value Proposition + Value Justification — Streamax

Value = Total Benefits - Total Investment. It must be QUANTIFIED and OWNED by the
buyer (their numbers), or it gets ignored and the deal stalls on price.

## Template (starts "We believe", becomes "You will" once they own it)
"We believe [fleet] should be able to [reduce at-fault accidents ~30% / cut
insurance premiums ~15% / eliminate $X in false-claim payouts] worth [$Y/year]
through the ability to [catch fatigue pre-event and prove not-at-fault instantly]
via [SafeGPT + AD Plus 2.0] for an investment of [~$Z]."

## Value Justification (at decision time)
Pick 2-3 elements that move the needle and get the buyer to supply the numbers:
- Revenue / profit impact.
- **Displaced cost** — e.g. retiring a separate GPS tracker (native CAN/OBD).
- **Avoided cost** — accidents, premiums, false-claim payouts you won't incur.
- Intangibles (only if the buyer assigns a value): driver retention, brand.
State the **payback month** (when cumulative benefit exceeds cumulative
investment). The buyer must be able to say "yes, those are MY numbers."

## Why it matters
Quantified value = a compelling reason to act (beats "no decision"), lets you
hold price in Phase III, and becomes the closing Value Proposition ("You WILL
reduce ... worth $Y, payback in N months").
