# Competitive Landscape — Battlecards and Trust Arguments

## Two categories of competitors — different conversations

| Category | Who | TSP's question | Your role |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The Enemy** | Samsara, Motive | "How can you help us **WIN** against them?" | Arm the TSP with pricing, features, positioning to beat them in the field |
| **Alternative Camera Partners** | Lytx Surfsight, MiTac/LightMetrics, Netradyne, Geotab GO Focus, Teltonika+Howen | "Why should we partner with Streamax **instead of** them?" | Show why Streamax is the better camera partnership |
| **Hardware Threats** | Hikvision, Jimilab, Concox | "They're cheaper / they have more distribution" | Product depth, platform, AI, connected advantage |

Never mix these conversations. The pitch is different.

**3-year TCO (SHAREABLE):**
- Motive: $70 × 36 = **$2,520/vehicle**
- Samsara: ~$1,590–1,690
- Streamax AD Plus via TSP: ~$1,100–1,280
- Streamax C6 Lite via TSP: ~$1,030–1,210

**Streamax is 50–59% cheaper than Motive over 3 years with MORE shipped safety features.** For a 500-truck fleet, that's $620K–$710K saved.

### Honest disclosure: single-lens vs Motive's dual-lens

**Do not volunteer this. If raised, handle as follows:**

Motive's dashcam uses dual lens: 40° telephoto for long-range + 120° wide. Streamax AD Plus 2.0/3.0 use single 4MP 130° wide. Detection range comparison (daytime):
- Traffic light: Streamax 8–30m vs Motive 8–45m
- License plate: Streamax 2–5m vs Motive 2–15m
- Stop/speed sign: Streamax 3–30m vs Motive 3–45m

Response:
1. **Acknowledge:** "Dual-lens provides an advantage for long-range sign and traffic light detection. That's a valid observation."
2. **Context:** "The features most affected — Red Light Violation and ALPR — are new features arriving Late 2026 for both of us. The safety-critical features that protect drivers today — fatigue, FCW, PCW — are not affected by this lens difference, and Motive doesn't have them yet."
3. **Redirect:** "Which features save lives today? Fatigue kills more truck drivers than any other factor. We detect it 15 minutes early. Motive doesn't detect it at all yet."
4. **Never deny.** Credibility > winning every feature point.

## vs. MiTac + LightMetrics (the most common alt camera partnership)

MiTac = contract camera ODM. LightMetrics = third-party AI as SaaS. Common bundled stack TSPs evaluate.

**The attack: third-party AI stack.**

> *"When your fleet customer has an AI accuracy issue — too many false alerts, missed detections, wrong classification — who fixes it? MiTac says it's a LightMetrics software problem. LightMetrics says it's a MiTac hardware limitation. You're stuck in the middle with an unhappy customer and no single point of accountability. With Streamax, one company owns hardware, firmware, AI, cloud platform. One call. One answer. One fix. **One throat to choke.**"*

Plus: MiTac doesn't read vehicle data → separate tracker required. Streamax CAN camera = one device.

## vs. Geotab GO Focus

Context: **Geotab IS a competing FM platform** with 2.6M subs. GO Focus runs MiTac hardware + Geotab's own AI (NOT LightMetrics).

**The trust kill:**

> *"Geotab is a fleet management platform with 2.6 million subscribers. They compete with you for FM customers. When you adopt GO Focus, you're building your video business on a competitor's camera optimised for a competitor's platform. Today they sell GO Focus through resellers. Tomorrow they offer Geotab+GO Focus as a direct bundle to your fleet customers — and you've helped them build the video offering that displaces you. Streamax is not a competing FM platform. We will never sell direct to your fleet customers."*

Plus: 5-year camera contract lock-in, 2-device model, $35–45/month to fleet.

## vs. Lytx Surfsight

Context: Lytx is #3 globally (1.3M subs, Q4 2025). Offers Surfsight as a resellable camera for TSPs. **Partnered with Geotab.**

**The trust kill (most powerful argument):**

> *"Lytx has partnered with Geotab. Geotab competes directly with you for fleet management customers. By choosing Lytx Surfsight, you're feeding your camera data into an ecosystem aligned with your competitor. Can you trust Lytx when they're in bed with Geotab? This is the Samsara/Motive playbook in slow motion. Today partnership. Tomorrow Lytx cameras pre-bundled with Geotab telematics direct to your fleet. You lose the video AND the tracking revenue. Streamax has no conflicting platform partnerships."*

Note: Lytx Surfsight is primarily an NA consideration. Outside NA this isn't usually a competitive situation.
