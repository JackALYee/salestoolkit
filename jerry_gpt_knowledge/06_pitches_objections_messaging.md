## SafeGPT messaging — say this / don't say this

**SAY:**
> *"Our cloud AI analyzes driving behavior continuously — not just individual events. It reduces alerts by 90% so your safety manager reviews 10–20 real risk events per day instead of 500. It catches fatigue 5–15 minutes before eyes close. Every alert comes with the risk classification and contributing data so your coaching conversations are based on facts, not opinions."*

**DON'T SAY:**
- Do NOT promise Evidence Cards — planned feature, not yet built. Say "on our roadmap" if asked.
- Do NOT claim specific accident reduction percentages unless backed by a customer case study.
- Do NOT disclose TOPS, chipset names, or AI model counts.
- Do NOT say "our cloud compensates for edge limitations" — we are not edge-disadvantaged.

## The Driver Buy-In Story (when fleet asks "how do I get my drivers to accept cameras?")

1. **Fewer false alerts = less frustration.** 90% fewer alerts means drivers aren't constantly beeped at for non-events. When the camera does alert, drivers know it's real.
2. **Transparent coaching = trust.** Every event comes with risk classification and contributing data. Coaching is specific and data-driven, not generic accusations.
3. **Targeted, not generic.** Each driver gets feedback on their specific patterns — not a one-size-fits-all lecture.
4. **Communication tool, not surveillance.** Two-way audio means the camera is the driver's tool too. TTS for route changes hands-free. No phone needed.
5. **The camera protects them.** When they're not at fault in an accident, video is the evidence that saves their career and their licence.

## The Top 15 Objections (and how to respond)

### 1. "Samsara has more AI models on the camera."
> *"Every major video telematics provider — including Samsara — uses a combination of edge and cloud processing. Not all of their claimed features run on the camera. We take the same approach: comprehensive edge detection for real-time alerts, plus SafeGPT cloud behavioral intelligence for pattern analysis and fatigue prediction. The result is 90% fewer alerts and fatigue detection 15 minutes before eyes close. The question isn't where the AI runs or how many models — it's what safety outcomes it delivers."*

### 2. "Your camera generates more events than Samsara — it's too noisy!"
> *"That's a design philosophy choice, not a defect. Samsara configures alerts as a fleet manager's tool — their following distance alert requires 60 seconds of continuous tailgating before triggering. That's a minute of dangerous driving before the driver gets warned. Our system triggers when time-to-impact drops below 0.6 seconds because we believe a safety alert should give the driver time to act — not document a near-miss after the fact. More events ≠ less accurate. It means the system is catching risks Samsara's configuration deliberately ignores. That said: our cameras are extremely configurable. If a fleet manager wants the Samsara approach, we support that too. The difference is we give you the choice. Samsara doesn't let you tighten their thresholds to protect drivers better."*

### 3. "Your platform isn't as mature as Samsara/Motive."
> *"We have the capabilities you need for your fleet today, and we're investing heavily in the platform. What we also offer is a willingness to work with you on specific requirements — we're not a take-it-or-leave-it vendor. Tell us what your fleet needs and we'll build it together."*

### 4. "You're a Chinese company — data security concerns."
> *"We host on AWS and OCI in France, UK, Brazil, Mexico, Australia, Japan, and Saudi Arabia. Your data stays in your country. We're GDPR compliant with privacy-by-design architecture. We manufacture in both China AND Vietnam for supply chain resilience. We have local subsidiaries in 8 countries with 180+ international staff. We're not asking you to trust a distant manufacturer — we're a local partner."*

### 5. "Your G-sensor harsh driving accuracy isn't good enough."
> *"We're actively improving our driving maneuver recognition algorithms. Our next-generation solutions will deliver significantly improved harsh driving accuracy through advanced sensor fusion — combining camera AI, CAN vehicle data, and G-sensor inputs for insurance-grade quality."*

### 6. "We already have a tracker, why do we need CAN in the camera?"

### 7. "Drivers won't accept cameras."

Coaching is specific to each driver's patterns, not generic. Two-way audio makes the camera a communication tool, not just surveillance. ## DSC vs DMS — the competitive kill shot (SHAREABLE)

> *"Samsara and Motive's fatigue detection runs on a wide-angle lens mounted above the driver's eyeline on the windshield. When a drowsy driver's head dips forward — the exact position you need to detect — their brow ridge covers the eyes from that downward camera angle. The AI can still catch yawning and head nodding, but it loses the most important indicator: the driver's eyes. Our C29N DMS camera mounts on the A-pillar at eye level, uses 940nm IR that works through sunglasses, and operates at 0 Lux. It never loses sight of the driver's eyes regardless of head position.*
>
> *Ask Samsara or Motive to run three separate fatigue-detection demos: (1) daytime with the driver wearing sunglasses, (2) night driving in low light, (3) the driver's head dipped slightly forward — the drowsy posture. Each one independently defeats a windshield-mounted dashcam: sunglasses block the eye signal, low light kills the standard IR, the dipped head puts the eyes behind the brow ridge. Then ask us — the C29N's A-pillar mount, 940nm IR through sunglasses, and 0-Lux capability handle all three scenarios."*

## Demo best practices

**For TSP partners:** Show the white-label platform with their logo. Demonstrate the API data feed. Show the margin calculator. Emphasise "every feature we build, you get automatically."

**For fleet operators:** Show the 90% alert reduction comparison (before/after). Show an early fatigue detection clip. Show the driver scorecard with behavioral tags. Show the two-way audio. Show the 15-minute installation video.

**The trap to avoid:** Do NOT demo feature-by-feature against a Samsara checklist. You will lose a feature count comparison. Instead, demo the WORKFLOW: *"Here's what your safety manager's morning looks like with Streamax"* — 10 events to review, each with context, coached in 20 minutes. vs. *"Here's what it looks like with 500 alerts."*

## "Lead with..." rules

- **Lead with the threat, not the technology** (TSP conversations — Samsara/Motive sell direct).
- **Lead with outcomes, not features** (fleet operator conversations).
- **Lead with TCO, not hardware price** (vs Hikvision/Jimilab and similar).
- **Lead with the market expects** (MDVR in LatAm/Africa/UK; dashcam in US/EU/Japan/ANZ).
- **Lead with C46A** for construction/forklift/agriculture; lead with C46 IPC/C53 for on-road and managed programs.
- **Lead with the business case, not the technology** when pitching TSP value prop (video doubles recurring revenue per vehicle, zero CAC).

## "Never say..." rules

- Never mention TOPS numbers (ours or competitors')
- Never say "our cloud compensates for edge limitations"
- Never say "we have fewer models" (we're closing the gap and will match within 6–12 months)
- Never get drawn into a model-by-model feature checklist comparison
- Never say "platform" to a TSP partner (say "Safety Intelligence Layer")
- Never promise Evidence Cards (say "on our roadmap")
- Never claim a specific accident-reduction % without a customer case study to back it
